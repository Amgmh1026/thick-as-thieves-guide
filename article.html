Thick As Thieves — Beginner's Guide

A friendly guide for new players: quick overview, first heist walkthrough, essential tips, and common mistakes.

Quick Overview

Thick As Thieves is a stealth-action game where you play as a thief planning and executing heists. Each level is a compact puzzle: you are given a target, a map, and a set of tools. Your job is to study the environment, avoid or neutralize guards and cameras, and extract the loot without triggering alarms. The game rewards careful observation, patient timing, and creative problem-solving. There are usually multiple valid approaches—sneak through vents, hack cameras, or make a bold distraction—so pick the style that fits you.

First Heist: Step-by-Step Walkthrough

This walkthrough covers the first mission, usually called the "Silent Heist." It’s designed to teach the basics while offering a satisfying challenge.

  1. Pause and survey the area. When the level begins, don’t move immediately. Use the camera or free-look to study guard routes, camera cones, and possible entry points. Identify a quiet path in and a backup escape route.
  2. Choose your loadout. For the first mission, a basic lockpick, a small distraction device (stone or coin), and a short-duration hacking tool are ideal. You won’t need everything—bring what fits your playstyle.
  3. Enter through the side alley. The front gate is heavily guarded; the side alley gives you a safer approach. Wait for the guard’s back to be turned, then slip past and enter quietly.
  4. Disable cameras smartly. Use the hacking tool on the nearest camera if it blocks your intended path. Don’t waste hacks—only disable cameras that directly threaten your route.
  5. Handle the alarm panel or lasers. If the vault is protected by a laser grid or alarm panel, find and hack the control box. The hack often has a cooldown, so move fast while it’s offline.
  6. Crack the vault carefully. When lockpicking is required, be patient: listen to audio cues and take your time with the mini-game. A fast failed attempt usually triggers the alarm.
  7. Exit with a plan. After grabbing the loot, retrace a low-risk path to the exit. If spotted, use your distraction or smoke device to buy time and reach cover. If the route is blocked, look for alternative exits such as vents or service doors.

5 Essential Tips for New Players

  • Be patient. Most mistakes happen when players rush. Observe patrol patterns and wait for clear windows to move.
  • Use sound as information. Footsteps, radios, and environmental noises reveal guard locations. Headphones help a lot.
  • Conserve your best tools. Save emergency gadgets (like smoke or long hacks) for chaotic moments where you must buy time.
  • Plan an escape early. Always have an exit strategy before attempting risky moves. Identify at least two escape options.
  • Learn from failure. If you trigger an alarm, replay the segment and note what gave you away. Each retry teaches you the map.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring cameras. It’s easy to focus on guards and miss cameras—check ceilings and corners for them.
  • Wasting items on trivial obstacles. Don’t burn your smoke or hacks on a single guard when sneaking past is possible.
  • Rushing lockpicks and hacks. Speed often causes failure. Slow, deliberate inputs reduce mistakes.
  • No backup plan. Many players get the loot but forget alternate exits—always map out a second route.
  • Overlooking audio cues. Radio chatter or a guard’s footsteps can tell you exactly when to move—ignore them at your peril.

Final Thoughts

Thick As Thieves is rewarding because it lets you be clever. There’s no single correct path; success comes from reading the level, staying calm, and making small, smart decisions. Try different approaches—silent runs, risky speedruns, or creative distractions—and see what fits you best. Most importantly, have fun. Every failed attempt is a lesson, and every clean run feels great.
